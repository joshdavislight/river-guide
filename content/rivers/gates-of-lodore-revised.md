The Gates of Lodore was named by John Wesley Powell on his legendary 1869 expedition through the canyons of the Green and Colorado Rivers. Today, this 43-mile section through Dinosaur National Monument offers solid Class III-IV whitewater with a longer, more reliable season than the nearby Yampa thanks to dam-controlled flows from Flaming Gorge Dam. Starting at the dramatic Gates of Lodore‚Äîa towering sandstone portal into Lodore Canyon‚Äîthe river flows through increasingly deep red rock canyon to Echo Park, where it meets the free-flowing Yampa River.

Lodore has a different character than the Yampa: dam-controlled flows mean cold, clear water (45-55¬∞F year-round) and predictable season extending into September. The canyon walls are dramatic Precambrian quartzite and sandstone rising 2,000+ feet above the river. The rapids are legitimate‚ÄîHell's Half Mile is a long, technical Class IV boulder garden that demands respect and careful scouting. The Jones Hole side hike to Fremont petroglyphs is a highlight, and Echo Park's confluence with the Yampa offers a powerful sense of the canyon system's scale and geologic history.

## When to Go

**May-June:** Higher flows (3,000-6,000 cfs) from dam releases coordinated with snowmelt. Bigger rapids, more powerful hydraulics. Cool temps (60-80¬∞F), lush vegetation.

**July-August:** More moderate flows (1,500-3,500 cfs). Warmer weather (75-95¬∞F) but water stays cold from dam. Best for families and intermediate boaters.

**September:** Lower flows (1,000-2,500 cfs), end of permit season. Cooling temps (65-85¬∞F), fewer crowds, beautiful light. Rapids become more technical with exposed rocks.

### When NOT to Go

**High water (above 6,000 cfs):** Hell's Half Mile becomes "super pushy" with boaters reporting getting "bounced down channels" and "pinballed through." At 8,000+ cfs, it transitions from technical Class IV to powerful big-water Class IV+. Multiple swims and flips occur every high-water season.

**Early May (below 1,500 cfs):** While still runnable, low flows expose rocks throughout Lodore Canyon making rapids more technical and boat-scraping more frequent. Hell's Half Mile becomes a tedious rock garden requiring constant maneuvering.

**Holiday weekends:** Memorial Day and July 4th see maximum crowding. Echo Park's limited camps get packed with multiple groups.

## Permit Strategy

At 10% odds (1 in 10), [Gates of Lodore has the best odds](https://www.recreation.gov/permits/250014) of any major whitewater permit in the Southwest. Tips:
- Apply in December-January via Recreation.gov  
- Good backup option if you don't win Yampa lottery
- If you win both Yampa and Lodore permits, combine them for an epic 12-14 day trip
- Weekday launches have slightly better odds than weekends

**Permit Realities:**
- **Significantly better odds than Yampa**: While the Yampa is 2.6% odds, Lodore is 10%‚Äînearly 4x better. Many groups apply for both.
- **Can combine with Yampa**: If you win both permits, you can run the Yampa from Deerlodge Park to Echo Park (71 miles), then continue down Lodore to Split Mountain (additional 19 miles from Echo Park). This creates an exceptional 12-14 day trip.
- **Shared takeout with Yampa**: Split Mountain is the standard takeout for both rivers, simplifying logistics if combining trips.
- **Dam flows = no snowpack gambling**: Unlike free-flowing rivers, you don't need to worry about drought years. Flaming Gorge Dam provides reliable flows all season.
