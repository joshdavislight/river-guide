# Rio Chama

The Rio Chama is New Mexico's only Wild and Scenic River, offering 31 miles of desert canyon floating through red rock walls and pi√±on-juniper forest. Dam-controlled flows from El Vado Dam create a more predictable season than most Southwestern rivers, but that predictability comes with serious permit penalties and flow frustrations.

This is the Southwest's most accessible permitted multi-day trip with friendly Class II-III whitewater, making it popular with intermediate paddlers and families. The challenge isn't the rapids‚Äîit's dealing with BOR's flow management and BLM's harsh permit policies.

## When to Go

### April (Late April Often Best)
No permit required before May 1, making late April the most popular launch window. Flows are typically good (800-1,500 cfs) from El Vado Dam releases. Water is cold from dam releases‚Äîdrysuits help. This is the "loophole" season that avoids lottery competition entirely.

### May-June (800-2,000 cfs)
Peak season with dam releases creating consistent flows. Water remains cold despite warm air temps. Memorial Day weekend is particularly crowded. This is when permits are required and competitive.

### July (400-1,000 cfs)
Late season as irrigation demands increase and releases decrease. Flows can drop to barely runnable levels. Some years the season ends by mid-July.

### When NOT to Go

**Low snow years:** El Vado Dam releases are entirely dependent on upstream snowpack. In drought years, BOR may provide minimal releases or shut off flows completely for downstream irrigation needs. The river can drop from 500 cfs to 80 cfs overnight without warning.

**August-September:** While technically the permit season extends through September, flows are typically too low by August. Irrigation demands drain the river to a trickle. Don't plan late-season trips without obsessively monitoring the gauge.

## Permit Strategy

The Rio Chama has one of the most peculiar permit systems in the West. Apply through the [BLM Rio Chama lottery](https://www.blm.gov/programs/recreation/permits-and-passes/lotteries-and-permit-systems/new-mexico/rio-chama) during January (postcard application by January 31st) for weekend launches May 1st onwards. Results come out around March 1st. Weekday launches are first-come, first-served with much better availability. Odds for weekend launches run around 20-30%.

**Permit Realities:**

**No permit needed before May 1st:** This is the critical loophole. Late April trips require no permit, no reservation, no lottery‚Äîjust show up and launch. This makes late April the most popular time to run the Chama, avoiding the entire permit system. Many experienced groups exclusively target the April 20-30 window.

**3-YEAR BAN for no-shows:** This is the harshest penalty in Western river permitting. If you win a permit and don't show up (without advance cancellation), BLM bans you from applying for 3 years. This isn't negotiable‚Äîmedical emergencies, car breakdowns, nothing excuses a no-show without prior notice. Call the BLM office if plans change.

**Dual agency confusion:** The upper 22 miles are BLM-managed (permit required during season), while the lower 9 miles are USFS (no permit ever). Different agencies, different policies for the same river trip. Most people ignore this distinction and just get the BLM permit.

**No cell service to check flows:** Once you commit and launch, you can't check the gauge. If BOR drops releases for irrigation mid-trip, you're committed to dragging boats through shallows. This happened to multiple groups in 2022 when flows dropped from 500 to 100 cfs overnight.
